# Roof module — Flat

> Module guide for the **flat** roof typology. It loads into the system prompt **only
> when the user picks the Flat roof** (or a structure that selects it). There is NO roof
> template — you build the cap yourself with ordinary ops, on top of `05-building-houses.md`.

## What a flat roof is

No pitch at all: the walls are capped by a **solid, walkable deck** with a slim **parapet**
rim around the edge. It reads modern, low and horizontal — and doubles as a roof terrace.
Because it leaves **no roof void**, a flat roof **cannot host an attic**.

## How to build it

1. **Cap with a deck.** `fill` a solid slab/block layer across the whole footprint at the
   top of the walls — the deck the player can stand on. Keep it level (no slope, no ridge).
2. **Add a parapet.** Run a **1-block-high** rim (`walls` of the wall/trim material, or a
   slab course) around the edge of the deck — the low lip that frames the terrace and stops
   the roof reading as an open box top.
3. **Keep the walls tall.** Unlike a pitched roof, a flat roof adds almost no height, so the
   storeys below it should rise nearly to the top of the box.
4. **No attic.** There's no void to floor — don't add an attic ladder or an in-roof room.
5. **Detail the terrace (optional).** Glass-pane or fence railings, planters, an access
   hatch/stair from the top storey, and recessed lighting all read well on a flat roof.

## Avoid

- Any pitch, ridge or gable end — that's a different roof typology.
- An open box top with no parapet (it reads unfinished).
- An attic or in-roof room (there is no void above a flat deck).
