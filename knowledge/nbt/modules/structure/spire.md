# Structure module — Spire

> Loads when the user picks the **Spire** (the second member of the `tower`
> family). A FRESH build is SEEDED with a code-built carved shell (see the "STARTING
> SHELL" preamble) — your job is to FINISH it, not reinvent it. The interior recipes live
> in `06-decoration-and-interiors.md` / `11-furniture-and-interior-detailing.md` and the
> furnishing-by-space rules in `14-furnishing-by-space.md`; this file is the
> spire-specific playbook.

## What a spire is

A derelict gothic **spire** — the brooding monolith of the references. Its character is in
the **carved silhouette**, not in sprawl: a battered, flared stone **plinth**; a vertically
**ribbed shaft** (organ-pipe buttress pilasters) that **steps inward in tiers** as it rises;
**iron-cage lantern arms** hanging on chains off the upper tiers; a **carved skull face**
(glowing socket eyes, a tooth row) on a wide front; full-height **corner buttress piers**
tipped with lit **spire finials**; soul-lit **lancet windows**; a pointed gothic **doorway**
under a glowing **inverted cross**; and a spiky **crenellated crown**. Cold blue soul-fire
light throughout. The wider the tower, the more ribs, arms and skull detail the shell carries.

The tower **owns its crown in code** — there is no Roof module slot and no Attic. The spiky
battlement + spire IS the keep's identity, so do NOT cap it with a pitched roof or a flat
terrace. (Basement, Surroundings and Room modules DO link to the tower.)

## What the seeded shell already gives you (KEEP it)

- The **flared plinth**, the **tiered ribbed shaft** (each tier stepped inward, with proud
  vertical buttress ribs), and the **setback ledge decks** that cover each step.
- **Corner buttress piers** rising past the crown to lit **spire finials**, a **central
  spire**, and a spiky **crenellated parapet** over a walkable roof deck.
- **Floor slabs** for every storey, a connected **stair core** (or a wall ladder), a **roof
  hatch** onto the crown, and a hanging **soul lantern under every ceiling**. Vertical
  circulation is ALREADY COMPLETE — every floor is linked and the crown is reachable. Do NOT
  add your OWN stairs or ladders, and **never build an exterior staircase up the outside of the
  shaft** (the entrance is the gothic doorway at the base). Extra/outside stairs are the exact
  "escada no exterior" defect to avoid; if a floor feels unreachable, it isn't — the core
  already serves it.
- The **soul-lit lancet windows**, the projecting **lantern-arm cages** on the upper tiers,
  the **carved skull face** (on a wide tower), and the **gothic doorway + inverted cross**.
- When picked: the **basement** (the chosen module's stone undercroft, barred — never glass —
  at any half-buried opening) reached by the central descent ladder, and the **surroundings**
  ring outside the walls.

Do NOT re-cap it, strip the parapet/spires, delete the stair core, fill in the skull eyes,
remove the hanging lantern arms, widen the lancet slits into picture windows, or open extra
holes in the shaft — the carved massing IS the tower.

## How to FINISH it

1. **Furnish floor-by-floor** per the floor plan: a sinister program per level (an entry
   hall / shrine at the base, a study or ritual chamber above, an observatory or roost at the
   top). Partition only a genuinely large floor; otherwise dress the single room. Light every
   level with soul fire; decoration goes in AIR cells — never overwrite the shell.
   **Respect the storey count — never add mezzanines.** The shell's floor slabs are the ONLY
   floors. A storey here is TALL on purpose (a cathedral-height tower volume); leave it as one
   open space with a high ceiling — hanging chandeliers/lanterns, tall lancet windows, a
   gallery of banners — and partition it only with WALLS within the same level. Do NOT insert
   an intermediate floor/deck/loft to "use up" the height: a half-height sub-level with a
   cramped ceiling is the exact defect to avoid, and it breaks the stair core that climbs
   slab-to-slab.
2. **Lean into the haunted mood**: cobwebs in the corners, candles and skulls on shelves,
   tattered banners, soul campfires/braziers, chains, a central altar or tomb, bookshelves and
   brewing stands for a sorcerer's spire. Keep the palette dark and cold-lit.
3. **The roof deck is open to the sky** between the merlons (don't roof it over) — add only
   flat-deck detail (a brazier, a beacon of soul fire, a banner, ravens).
4. **Keep the lancet slits narrow** and the **skull eyes glowing** — they're the tower's face.

## Pairs well with

The **Haunted** decoration (its decay weathers and chips the shaft). A **Crypt** or **Cult
temple** basement makes it a true cursed sanctum; a **Graveyard** surroundings ring sets it in
a fog-bound cemetery.
