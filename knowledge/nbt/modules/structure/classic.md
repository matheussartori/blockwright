# Structure module — Classic house

> Loads when the user picks the **Classic** house (a member of the `house` family). A
> FRESH build is SEEDED with a code-built shell (see the "STARTING SHELL" preamble) —
> your job is to FINISH it, not reinvent it. Pairs by default with the **Cozy**
> decoration. The interior recipes live in `06-decoration-and-interiors.md` /
> `11-furniture-and-interior-detailing.md`; this file is the classic-specific playbook.

## What a classic house is

A storeyed home with a single pitched roof, framed corner posts, banded symmetric
windows, a centred front door on a stone plinth, and one complete chimney. Its character
is calm massing with the detail in the roof and facade. **No two shells are alike**: the
seed varies the window rhythm (single / paired / tall), the corner treatment, the roof
form (gable either way, or hip) and the chimney side run-to-run — the variety is already
in the shell you receive.

## What the seeded shell already gives you (KEEP it)

- The **foundation slab**, the four-sided wall shell, the **stone plinth** course, and
  (per seed) framed **corner posts**.
- A **single pitched roof** (gable with end vents, or hip — honouring a picked roof
  form), guaranteed to cap the box. The **attic loft**, when picked, is already floored
  and lit inside it.
- **Floor slabs** for every storey, a **switchback stair core** linking every walkable
  level (a ladder up to the attic), and a hanging **lantern under every ceiling**.
- The **seated front door** with a lantern just inside, the symmetric **window bands**
  per facade, and **one chimney** column rising through the roof on its seeded side.
- When picked: the **basement** (the cellar module's stone undercroft, barred — never
  glass — at any half-buried clerestory) and the **recessed covered balcony**.

Do NOT re-roof it, strip the corner posts, delete the stair core, open extra holes in the
shell, or add a second chimney — the massing IS the classic house.

## How to FINISH it

1. **Furnish room-by-room** per the floor plan: partition each storey into real, separated
   spaces (interior walls with doorways), then a hearth at the chimney's base, table +
   chairs, beds upstairs, storage, rugs. Light every room; decoration goes in AIR cells —
   never overwrite the shell.
2. **Finish the hearth**: the chimney column lands at the ground floor — build the firebox
   /fireplace around its base and cap the stack with a `campfire` resting on the top block.
3. **Dress the openings in air cells**: sills (slabs) and lintels (trapdoors/stairs)
   AROUND the window panes, a 1–2 block stoop and a framed porch overhang at the door —
   added beside/over the shell, never by carving it.
4. **Ground it**: a path to the door, flower boxes under windows, a low garden edge, ivy
   on a corner — so the house sits in a place rather than floating. (A picked Garden
   surroundings ring is part of the shell — only add small outdoor detail on it.)

## Common classic-house mistakes

- Re-emitting the exterior "improved" — a furniture-only patch is the right move for
  detail passes; the shell is restored if deleted, so fighting it just wastes rounds.
- A second/stray chimney, or a campfire floating above the stack instead of resting on it.
- Bare doorways or windows with no surrounding trim — dress them in adjacent air cells.
- Forgetting interior light → dark, dead rooms in the preview.
- Decoration that overwrites walls/floors: appliances keep the wall block behind them and
  face into the room.
