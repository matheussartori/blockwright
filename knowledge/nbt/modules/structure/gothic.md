# Structure module — Gothic

> Loads when the user picks **Gothic**. A FRESH build is SEEDED with a code-built shell
> (see the "STARTING SHELL" preamble) — your job is to FINISH it, not reinvent it. Pairs by
> default with the **Gothic decoration** (black timber + blackstone, pale stone detailing).

## What a gothic house is

A **dark manor**: **predominantly black** (dark-oak boarding + blackstone) **picked out with
pale stone detailing** (white belt courses, sills, jambs). A **steep dark slate roof**.
The signature moves from the references: a **central frontispiece tower** projecting at the
front-centre and rising **past the ridge** to a steep pointed cap + lit finial (it carries
the **grand arched entrance**), a **covered front veranda** (a balustraded colonnade across
the facade), a **mini corner tower** rising past the roofline to a pointed stepped cap +
finial, a **glass chapel wing** down one side (a wall of tall grey windows), and **ivy
garlands** draping the eaves + tower shoulders. Brooding, vertical, asymmetric, soul-lit.

## What the seeded shell already gives you (KEEP it)

- The **black shell** with **pale white belt courses** at each floor line + a cornice, and
  dark-log corner quoins.
- A **steep slate gable/hip roof** (respecting the chosen roof form).
- The **central frontispiece tower**: a square turret projecting at the front-centre, rising
  past the ridge to a steep pyramidal cap + a soul-lit finial, with tall grey lancet windows
  and the **grand arched entrance** on its front face. The dominant vertical accent — KEEP it.
- The **covered front veranda**: blackstone columns carrying a flat roof across the facade,
  a pale lintel, hung soul-lanterns, with a **fence balustrade** between the columns.
- The **mini corner tower** (a hollow shaft past the roofline, window slits, a stepped
  pointed cap + a soul-lit finial).
- The **glass chapel wing**: the left side re-glazed into **grey-glass windows** framed by
  pale mullions. **Ivy garlands** dotting the eaves + tower shoulders. A working
  **staircase** between floors.

Do NOT make it symmetric/squat, strip the central tower / corner tower / chapel glass, drop
the veranda or the ivy, lighten the walls, or shallow the roof — they ARE the gothic manor.

## How to FINISH it

1. **Sharpen the verticality** — add finials/spikes along the ridge and parapet, taller
   pointed window heads, a second smaller pinnacle.
2. **Furnish** the chapel side as a tall hall (pews/altar/candles) and the rooms as a manor;
   keep the soul-lantern cold light.
3. **Detail the portico** — an iron-bar/fence balustrade between columns, a stepped entry,
   wall sconces.
4. **Exterior gloom:** dead bushes, a fence + gate, cobwebs in the eaves, a smoking chimney.

## Avoid

- A warm or light palette — keep it **black with white detailing** only.
- A squat symmetric box or a shallow roof — gothic is **tall, sharp, asymmetric**; the
  **tower + portico + glass chapel** must all read.
