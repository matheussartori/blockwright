# Structure module — L-shaped house

> Loads when the user picks **L-shaped house**. A FRESH build is SEEDED with a code-built
> shell (see the "STARTING SHELL" preamble) — your job is to FINISH it, not reinvent it.

## What an L-shaped house is

A house whose footprint is an **L**: a long **main wing** plus a perpendicular **back wing**
meeting at the back corner, leaving a **front-right notch** that reads as a sheltered
**terrace/courtyard** in the crook. Two pitched roofs, one per wing. More character than a
single box.

## Finishing the seeded shell

The shell gives you the L footprint, perimeter walls, the two gable roofs, and a railed
terrace deck in the notch — KEEP all of it. Add:

1. **Furnish both wings** as separated rooms (the main wing and the back wing read as
   different spaces — e.g. living/kitchen in one, bedrooms in the other), in the chosen
   decoration's materials.
2. **Use the terrace** in the inner corner: a table and chairs, planters, a lantern, steps
   down to the garden, a path.
3. **Exterior detail:** flower boxes, bushes/hedges along the facade, accent trim at the
   wing junction, a chimney finished off.
4. **Circulation:** link the floors with a clean stair (or a flush ladder); the wings can
   share one stair core.

## Avoid

- Filling the notch back into a plain rectangle — the L (and its terrace) must stay.
- One continuous flat lid over both wings — keep the two pitched roofs.
