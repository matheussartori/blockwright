# Structure module — Cabin

> Loads when the user picks **Cabin**. A FRESH cabin build is SEEDED with a code-built
> shell (see the "STARTING SHELL" preamble) — your job is to FINISH it, not reinvent it.

## What a cabin is

A rustic log-and-stone cabin: raised on a **stone plinth**, **log corner posts** and timber
walls, a **steep gable roof with deep eaves**, a **covered front porch** on log posts with
fence railings and steps, and a **stone chimney**. Cozy, woodsy, lived-in.

## Finishing the seeded shell

The shell gives you the raised base, log-framed walls, steep roof, porch and chimney —
KEEP all of it. Add:

1. **Furnish the interior** warmly: a hearth/fireplace at the chimney, a seating nook, a
   small kitchen and beds, shelves, wool rugs, lanterns and candles.
2. **Dress the porch:** a bench or chairs, a barrel/lantern, planters, a doormat (carpet).
3. **Exterior detail:** a woodpile, flower boxes under windows, leaf/vine accents, a stone
   path to the steps, bushes — the cozy survival-home touch.
4. **Circulation:** for two floors, a simple wood stair or a flush ladder.

## Avoid

- Flattening the roof or removing the porch / stone base (those define the cabin).
- Re-cladding it in white concrete/quartz (that's the modern look).
- Leaving the timber meeting the dirt — the stone plinth stays.
