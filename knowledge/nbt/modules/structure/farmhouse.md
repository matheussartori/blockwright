# Structure module — Farmhouse

> Loads when the user picks **Farmhouse**. A FRESH build is SEEDED with a code-built shell
> (see the "STARTING SHELL" preamble) — your job is to FINISH it, not reinvent it. Pairs by
> default with the **Farmhouse decoration** (warm oak + dark slate roof).

## What a farmhouse is

A rustic country *casa de sítio* — **never a plain rectangle**. Its footprint is an **L**: a
long **main wing** plus a perpendicular **back wing**, so the roofline is a **cross-gable**
(two ridges meeting at right angles, the farmhouse "H"). Across the main wing's front runs a
**deep covered veranda** on timber posts with an **upper external gallery** over it; a
sheltered **covered porch** sits in the crook (the notch). Honey oak boarding crossed by
dark stripped-log framing over a stone plinth, a steep dark tiled roof, a gable-end chimney.

## What the seeded shell already gives you (KEEP it)

- The **L footprint** + cross-gable roof (respecting the chosen gable/hip/flat form).
- The **covered front veranda**: a full-height colonnade of dark-log posts, the ground
  facade stepped back behind it (a covered porch), a balustrade, a central entry with a
  timber **portal lintel**, porch "chair" stairs and hung lanterns.
- The **upper external gallery**: a railed walkway over the porch roof with doors onto it.
- The **covered porch in the notch**, the **stone plinth**, the **exposed framing**, the
  **gable-end chimney stack**, and a working **staircase** between floors.

Do NOT flatten the L back into a box, re-roof it, strip the veranda/gallery/framing, or
remove the stairs — they ARE the farmhouse.

## How to FINISH it

1. **Wrap the veranda further** — carry the colonnade/roof from the main-wing front around a
   side or further into the notch so the porch reads as a true wraparound; add rest areas
   (benches, tables, potted plants, hay bales).
2. **Furnish room-by-room**, partitioning the wings into separate spaces, and make sure each
   upper room has a door onto the gallery so it links them.
3. **Cap the chimney** with a smoking `campfire` and finish the hearth at its base inside.
4. **Exterior detail:** flower boxes under the wide ground windows, ivy on corners, a path
   and steps up to the porch, dormers on the slopes, garden hedges.

## Avoid

- A single rectangular box — the L + cross-gable + veranda are the whole identity.
- A light/thatch roof, or overriding the chosen roof form.
- Tiny single windows downstairs — farmhouse ground windows are wide and banded.
