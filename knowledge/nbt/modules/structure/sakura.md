# Structure module — Sakura house

> Loads when the user picks **Sakura**. A FRESH build is SEEDED with a code-built shell
> (see the "STARTING SHELL" preamble) — your job is to FINISH it, not reinvent it. Pairs by
> default with the **Sakura decoration** (pink cherry cladding + a pink roof).

## What a sakura house is

A **cherry-blossom cottage RAISED on a visible stone-brick basement** — the base reads as a
storey from outside (low **barred** openings, a half-buried look), not a hidden cellar. The **main
entrance sits up on the raised floor**, reached by an **exterior stone stair** that climbs in
under the **overhanging upper storey** (a covered, recessed entry). Soft **pink cherry-wood
cladding** above the stone base, a **pink cherry-stair gable roof** crowned with **blossom
cascades** (cherry-leaf foliage), **leafy window boxes**, and an **upper-front balcony**.
Romantic, springtime, a house tucked in a grove of cherry trees.

## What the seeded shell already gives you (KEEP it)

- The **visible stone-brick basement** storey with small `iron_bars` vents (basement openings
  are ALWAYS iron bars, never glass) + the raised cherry floor.
- The **exterior stone stair** climbing up to a **recessed, covered front door** under the
  upper-floor overhang (with timber overhang posts + a hung lantern).
- The **pink cherry cladding**, cherry-log corner posts, and a **pink cherry-stair roof**.
- The **blossom crown**: a `cherry_leaves` garland along the front eave + **cascades down the
  front corners**, plus **leafy window boxes** under the windows.
- The **upper-front balcony** rail, and a working **staircase** between the cherry floors.

Do NOT bury the basement, flatten it to grade, move the door down to ground level, re-roof it
a dark/red colour, or strip the blossoms/balcony — they ARE the sakura house.

## How to FINISH it

1. **Furnish the raised floors** room-by-room; keep the basement as a usable lower room
   (storage, a cozy den) lit through its barred vents (`iron_bars`, never glass panes).
2. **Extend the blossom** — drape more `cherry_leaves` boughs over the eaves and around the
   garden, add `pink_petals`/flower pots, hanging lanterns from the balcony.
3. **Dress the entry stair** — widen/landing it, add a fence rail and planters flanking it.
4. **Exterior detail:** window-box flowers, a small cherry tree or two beside the house, a
   stone path up to the stair, a tea-table on the balcony.

## Avoid

- A flat, leafless box at grade — the **raised stone base + the climb to a covered upper
  entry + the blossoms** are the whole identity; keep all three.
- A bright-red or dark roof — keep it **soft pink** (cherry) so the blossom reads.
