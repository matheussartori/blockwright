# Structure module — Church

> Module guide for the **church** structure type. It loads into the system prompt only when
> the church is the selected structure. The church is a CODE-BUILT, locked shell: a fresh
> build is seeded with its compiled exterior (nave + steep roof + buttresses + arched
> windows + front bell tower with a spire and cross), and you only FURNISH the interior and
> add fine exterior detail. Do NOT re-roof, re-clad, or demolish the shell — every solid
> exterior cell is preserved by the compiler.

## What the code builds (KEEP it — it's locked)

- **A long nave** under a steep **gabled roof** (the ridge runs the long axis; the roof is a
  dark `*_stairs` material). The roof covers the whole footprint.
- **Buttress pilasters** — dressed-stone strips spaced along the long eave walls.
- **Tall arched windows** between the buttresses (glass with a stone sill + an accent head).
- **A square front BELL TOWER** seated on the front wall, rising CLEAR of the nave ridge to a
  **stepped pyramidal spire topped by a cross**, with a louvered belfry and a bell lantern.
- **An arched front portal** at the tower base (a double door with stone jambs + a keystone).
- A **ladder up the tower** to the belfry; a **stair core** only when a gallery floor exists.

## What YOU add (the interior + dressing)

- **A central aisle of pews** facing the altar — stairs or slabs in two banks down the nave.
- **A raised altar at the far (back) end**: a lectern/enchanting-table or a few steps up to a
  table with candles or lanterns, gold/quartz trim, a banner or painting behind it.
- **A font or organ** near the entrance; **chandeliers** (lanterns on chains) down the nave.
- **Stained-glass accents** in the tall windows are welcome (red/blue/white) — but never
  delete the window opening; recolour the existing glass solid→solid.
- Keep the nave **open, tall and symmetric** — it is one program (one room per floor).

## Materials & mood

- The decoration supplies the palette — **chapel** (white plaster over stone, dark roof) by
  default, or **castle** (all-grey stone). Keep added surfaces in that family: white walls
  framed by stone, dark deepslate roof, dark-oak woodwork, lanterns/sea-lanterns for light.
- **Verticality and light** are the church's mood: long lit windows, a tall nave, the steeple
  reaching highest. Don't clutter the silhouette — let the tower and ridge read clean.
- Pairs naturally with a **crypt** basement (a vaulted tomb below) and a **graveyard** or
  **garden** surroundings (a fenced churchyard).
