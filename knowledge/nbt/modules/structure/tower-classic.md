# Structure module — Classic tower (keep)

> Loads when the user picks the **Classic** tower (the first member of the `tower`
> family). A FRESH build is SEEDED with a code-built shell (see the "STARTING SHELL"
> preamble) — your job is to FINISH it, not reinvent it. The interior recipes live in
> `06-decoration-and-interiors.md` / `11-furniture-and-interior-detailing.md` and the
> furnishing-by-space rules in `14-furnishing-by-space.md`; this file is the
> tower-specific playbook.

## What a classic tower is

A battlemented stone **keep**: a tall, square stone shaft of stacked narrow storeys,
arrow-slit windows, a seated arched doorway on a stone plinth, a connected switchback
stair core (a flush wall ladder on the tightest footprints), and a **crenellated
parapet** — merlons with crenel gaps — crowning a walkable roof deck. Its character is
vertical, defensive, and stony: the detail is in the silhouette (the merlons, the slit
windows, the heavy base), not in sprawl.

The tower **owns its crown in code** — there is no Roof module slot. The battlement is
the keep's identity, so do NOT replace it with a pitched roof, a spire, or a flat
terrace. (Basement, Surroundings and Room modules DO link to the tower.)

## What the seeded shell already gives you (KEEP it)

- The **foundation slab**, the four-sided stone shaft, and a heavier **stone plinth**
  course at the ground base.
- A solid **roof deck** capping the shaft and a **crenellated parapet** (merlons + crenel
  gaps) one course above it — the walkable battlement.
- **Floor slabs** for every storey, a connected **stair core** (or a wall ladder) linking
  every walkable level, and a hanging **lantern under every ceiling**.
- The **seated front door** with a lantern just inside, and **arrow-slit windows** (narrow
  2-tall slits) centred on each wall, per storey.
- When picked: the **basement** (the chosen module's stone undercroft, barred — never
  glass — at any half-buried opening) reached by the central descent ladder, and the
  **surroundings** ring outside the walls.

Do NOT re-cap it (no second roof, no spire), strip the parapet, delete the stair core,
widen the slit windows into picture windows, or open extra holes in the shaft — the
massing IS the keep.

## How to FINISH it

1. **Furnish floor-by-floor** per the floor plan: one program per cramped level reads best
   (a guard room / armoury at the base, a study or bedchamber above, a lookout or storage
   at the top). Partition only if a level is genuinely large; otherwise dress the single
   room. Light every level; decoration goes in AIR cells — never overwrite the shell.
2. **Lean into the keep mood**: banners flanking the entrance, a weapon rack or barrels at
   the base, a writing desk / bookshelves higher up, a brazier or campfire on the roof
   deck behind the merlons, lanterns or torches on the stair landings.
3. **The roof deck is a fighting platform** — keep it OPEN to the sky between the merlons
   (don't roof it over); add only flat-deck detail (a brazier, a flagpole banner, chests).
4. **Keep the slit windows narrow** — they're arrow loops, not glazing. A pane or two is
   enough; do not enlarge them.

## Pairs well with

Any decoration (the stone shell reads under all of them); the **Haunted** look turns it
into a ruined watchtower (its decay weathers and chips the shaft). A **Crypt** or
**Cult temple** basement makes a sinister keep; a **Garden** or **Graveyard** surroundings
ring grounds it in the landscape.
