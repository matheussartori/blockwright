# Exterior module — Farmhouse

> Module guide for the **farmhouse** exterior style. It loads into the system prompt
> **only when the user picks the Farmhouse exterior**. A farmhouse is a *casa de sítio*,
> never the plain rectangular box of the classic shell — so this style is **CODE-BUILT and
> SEEDED**: when it's selected you are handed a STARTING SHELL that already has the deep
> covered front veranda on timber posts, the entry "portal", the upper external gallery, the
> exposed dark-log framing, the stone plinth, the dark tiled roof with dormers, and a working
> staircase. **Your job is to KEEP and EXTEND that shell — not rebuild it.** It does NOT
> change the interior program (that's the rooms).

## What is already built for you (keep it)

The seeded shell gives you, on the FRONT:
- a **deep covered veranda** — a 2-storey colonnade of `stripped_dark_oak_log` posts with the
  ground facade set back behind it (a covered porch, roofed by the upper floor) and a fence
  balustrade flanking a central entry;
- a **timber entry "portal"** (post jambs + a beam lintel) over the front door;
- an **upper external gallery** — a railed walkway over the porch roof with doors from the
  front rooms onto it;
- **exposed timber framing** (corner posts + a mid-wall belt + battens) over a 2-course
  **cobblestone plinth**;
- a **steep dark `deepslate_tile` roof** with **dormers** poking out of the front slope;
- the **stairs** linking the floors.

Do NOT flatten this back into a box, re-roof it, or strip the veranda/gallery/framing — they
ARE the style. Build ONLY on top of it.

## How to EXTEND it (your job)

1. **Wrap the porch onto the sides.** The seeded veranda is front-only — carry the colonnade
   and roof around **one or both side walls** so it reads as a true wraparound (the *grande
   corredor externo coberto*). Add **rest areas**: stair "chairs", trapdoor tables, benches,
   flower pots, lanterns — facing the yard.
2. **Furnish the upper gallery** and make sure each upper room has a door onto it, so it
   genuinely **links the bedrooms** (*corredor superior interligando os quartos*).
3. **Break the massing further.** If there's room, push the entry bay or a side wing
   **forward** (a projecting gabled bay) so the footprint reads cross-wing / **H / T plan**,
   not one rectangle. Off-set or set-back the upper floor.
4. **Big banded ground-floor windows.** Widen the ground windows into **2–3-pane bands** with
   slab sills and `spruce_trapdoor` / `dark_oak` shutters — never tiny single panes.
5. **Furnish the interior** room-by-room and fix anything unsound.

## Roof — dark, steep, and SHAPED, but honour the user's choice

- **Material:** dark and tiled — `deepslate_tile_stairs` (with a `deepslate_tile_slab`
  ridge), **steep**, with a clear **1-block eave overhang** + fascia. Never thatch or a
  light roof.
- **Form follows the user's Roof selection.** The user picks **gable / hip / flat** — that
  is the BASE roof form and you must respect it. The farmhouse "shaped roof" is what you
  layer ON TOP of that base, NOT a replacement:
  - **gable** (the typical farmhouse): give each wing its OWN gable ridge so the roofline
    reads as an **H / cross-gable** of several intersecting pitches; add **dormers** poking
    out of the slope and a small **cross-gable over the entry portico**.
  - **hip:** keep hipped slopes but still break them per wing, with hipped dormers.
  - **flat:** keep the flat deck the user asked for — express the "farmhouse" through the
    framing, plinth, porch and chimney instead; do **not** add a pitched roof over it.
- So: **alternative roof *shapes* = multiple intersecting ridges + dormers**, always built
  out of the user's chosen base form. Don't override a hip with a gable, or roof a flat.

## Chimney — tall and exterior

A prominent **exterior chimney** climbing a **gable end** (or the junction between two
wings), in `bricks` / `cobblestone` / `deepslate`, standing proud of the wall and rising a
couple of blocks above the ridge with a smoking `campfire` at its base inside. Position it
where it reads against the sky — a corner or gable end, not buried mid-roof. One chimney.

## Materials (skin)

- **Walls:** `oak_planks`, framed by `stripped_dark_oak_log` posts + beams (Tudor-style).
- **Framing:** `stripped_dark_oak_log` vertical posts at corners + quarter points and a
  horizontal **belt course** at mid-wall, splitting the facade into two registers.
- **Porch posts / gallery posts:** `stripped_dark_oak_log` (or `oak_log`).
- **Plinth / base:** a 2-course `cobblestone` water table so timber never meets soil.
- **Roof:** `deepslate_tile_stairs` + `deepslate_tile_slab` ridge.
- **Windows:** `glass_pane`, wide bands, slab sills, `spruce_trapdoor` shutters.
- **Door:** `oak_door` under the porch portico.
- **Rails:** `oak_fence` (porch balustrade + upper gallery).
- **Greenery:** ivy (`vine`) trailing down corners, **flower boxes** under windows, potted
  plants on the porch — the lived-in note from the references.
- **Light:** hung `lantern` along the porch and gallery.

## Signature volumes — seeded; KEEP, then EXTEND

1. **Covered front veranda** on posts with the entry portal — *seeded*; wrap it onto a side + furnish it.
2. **Upper external gallery** linking the rooms — *seeded*; furnish + connect every upper room's door.
3. **Exposed timber framing** + **deep stone plinth** — *seeded*; keep, don't strip.
4. **Dark slate roof with dormers** — *seeded*; keep the form, optionally add cross-gables.
5. **Cross-wing / H massing** — push a wing/bay forward if there's room (your addition).
6. **Tall exterior chimney** — seeded on a side wall; you may raise/relocate it to a gable end (keep ONE).

## Avoid

- Flattening the seeded shell back into a plain box, or stripping the veranda/gallery/framing.
- Re-roofing it, or overriding the user's chosen roof form (gable/hip/flat) — keep it.
- A flush, single-material wall — the framing + plinth + porch must read.
- Tiny single windows — ground windows are wide and banded.
- Leaving the porch a bare awning — it must keep its posts, deck, railing, seating and roof.
