# Exterior module — Farmhouse

> Module guide for the **farmhouse** exterior style. It loads into the system prompt
> **only when the user picks the Farmhouse exterior**. An exterior style is the house's
> OUTSIDE finish — its cladding, trim, roof colour and window treatment — plus a couple of
> signature added volumes. It does NOT change the interior program (that's the rooms) or
> the surroundings. Build it yourself with ordinary ops on top of `05-building-houses.md`.

## The look

A warm, rustic country home: honey-toned **oak boarding** crossed by darker **exposed
timber framing**, a **dark slate roof**, a grounded **stone plinth**, and **big banded
windows**. The storybook farmhouse — cosy, framed, solid.

## Materials (skin)

- **Walls:** `oak_planks`, framed by `stripped_dark_oak_log` posts + beams (Tudor-style).
- **Corners / framing:** `stripped_dark_oak_log` (vertical posts) and a horizontal
  **belt course** of the same log at mid-wall height, splitting the facade into two
  registers.
- **Plinth / base:** a 2-course `cobblestone` water table at the bottom so timber never
  meets soil.
- **Roof:** dark and tiled — `deepslate_tile_stairs` (with `deepslate_tile_slab` ridge),
  steep, with a **1-block eave overhang** + fascia.
- **Windows:** generous — wide 2–3 pane bands, `glass_pane`, often with a slab sill and
  shutters (`spruce_trapdoor` / `dark_oak` accents) framing them.
- **Door:** `oak_door` under a small **covered porch** (a 2-deep roofed stoop on posts).
- **Light:** `lantern`.

## Signature volumes

1. **Exposed timber framing.** Run vertical `stripped_dark_oak_log` posts at the quarter
   points of every wall and a horizontal belt course at mid-height — the dark frame
   reading against the oak boards is the whole farmhouse signature.
2. **Cross-gable entry.** A small **projecting gabled porch** over the front door (its own
   little pitched roof facing front), supported on two posts — a covered stoop.
3. **Deep stone plinth.** The visible cobblestone base course wrapping the whole house.

## Avoid

- A flush, single-material box — the framing + plinth + porch are what make it read.
- A light or thatch roof — keep it **dark and tiled**.
- Tiny single windows — farmhouse windows are wide and banded.
