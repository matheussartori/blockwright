# Room module — Living room

> Furniture vocabulary for this room. Scale, preset, and decoration come from the
> `[Room plan]` brief + `14-furnishing-by-space.md`; here is just WHAT to build.

The living room is the social heart of the home — the first room a guest sees. Build it
around a focal point and leave real walking space (never a furniture-packed cube).

**Focal point — pick one:**
- A **fireplace**: a 1–2 wide hearth recessed in a wall, `campfire` or `netherrack`+fire
  on a non-flammable surround (`brick`/`stone_brick`/`deepslate`), a mantel of stairs/slabs,
  a flue going up (if this is the chimney floor). Keep flammable blocks one cell clear.
- A large **window** wall, or a bookshelf-and-painting feature wall.

**Seating cluster** facing the focal point:
- "Sofas" / armchairs from **stairs** with **trapdoor** or sign armrests; or `*_stairs`
  rows. A 2–3 seat couch + one or two chairs.
- A **coffee table**: a fence/`*_fence` post + carpet/pressure plate top, or a slab on
  short supports. Add a `flower_pot`, a `lantern`, or a candle.
- A **rug**: a rectangle of `carpet` (a contrasting border colour reads as a real rug).

**Dressing:** wall **paintings** (item frames or `painting`), `bookshelf` or `chiseled_bookshelf`
units, a `potted` plant in a corner, wall sconces. Light it with `lantern`s, hanging
lanterns, or candles — warm and visible, never a dark room.
