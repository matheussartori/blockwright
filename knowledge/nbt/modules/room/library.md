# Room module — Library

> Furniture vocabulary for this room. Scale, preset, and decoration come from the
> `[Room plan]` brief + `14-furnishing-by-space.md`; here is just WHAT to build.

A library is a quiet, book-lined reading room. The defining move is **walls of shelving**;
fill the perimeter and let a reading setup sit in the middle.

**Shelf walls:**
- Line the walls **floor-to-ceiling** with `bookshelf` / `chiseled_bookshelf` (mix the
  two so the wall isn't uniform). Leave gaps for windows and the door.
- Break long runs with a **ladder** or a short **stair flight** up to a higher shelf tier
  if the ceiling is tall, or with a glassed **cabinet** (a `barrel`/framed display).
- A `lectern` (with a book) mounted against a shelf reads as a catalogue stand.

**Reading centre:**
- A **study table**: slabs or a full table with **lecterns** and **candles** on top, a
  globe suggested by a `chain`+`end_rod` or a flower pot.
- One or two **reading chairs** (stairs + trapdoor arms) by a window, a small side table
  with a `lantern` or candle.
- A **rug** under the table (carpet rectangle with a border).

**Light:** a centre **chandelier** (a ring of `lantern`s / candles on a `fence`+`chain`
frame, or hanging lanterns), plus shelf-level candles. Keep it warm and even — reading
light, not gloom. Floors in dark `*_planks` or polished stone suit the studious mood.
