# Room module — Morgue

> Furniture vocabulary for this room. Scale, preset, and decoration come from the
> `[Room plan]` brief + `14-furnishing-by-space.md`; here is just WHAT to build.

A morgue is where the dead are kept and prepared. The defining moves are **cold slabs with
sheeted bodies**, a **wall of cadaver drawers**, and an **embalming station** — clinical,
even light and hard, cold materials.

**Slabs (the cold tables):**
- A **slab** is a waist-high table: `smooth_stone`/`quartz`/`polished_diorite` top on a
  stone base, often a `*_slab` surface so it reads thin and surgical.
- Lay a **sheeted body** on some of them — a `white`/`light_gray` carpet or wool blanket
  shaped to a form (a slight `slab` lump for the head/feet). Hang a `lantern` over each and
  set a grated drain (`iron_bars`/`grate`) beneath.

**Cadaver drawers:**
- A **grid of fronted compartments** on a wall — `*_trapdoor`s as drawer faces over a frame,
  or `iron`/quartz blocks with `tripwire_hook`/button handles. Leave one or two **ajar** (an
  open trapdoor with a sheet edge showing).

**Embalming station:**
- A long table of **bottles and fluids** (`*_potion`/`glass` in `item_frame`s, `brewing_stand`s),
  an **instrument tray** (shears/flint in frames), a **wash basin** (`cauldron` with water),
  and a `composter`/barrel for waste.

**Storage of the dead:**
- **Coffins** on trestles (a `dark_oak`/`spruce` box, `chest`-and-slab, or stairs+slabs
  forming the casket) and a **stack of spare lids** leaned aside.

**Scale up:** a full **wall of drawers**, several slabs in a grid each with its own lamp and
drain, glass display **cabinets** of specimen jars (organs/oddities), and a registrar's
**desk** with a ledger (`lectern`+book) and candle.

**Light:** cold, even, STERILE — `lantern`s behind `iron_bars`/frosted grilles, or
`sea_lantern`/`white` light, not warm candlelight. Floor in `polished` stone or quartz with a
central grated channel; tiled walls (`stone_bricks`/`quartz_bricks`).
