# Room module — Kitchen

> Loads **only when the user assigns a Kitchen to a floor**. Guidance-only: build the
> furnishing yourself with ordinary ops inside the floor's interior (the "[Room plan]"
> lines give the floor's y range). Share the storey with the other assigned room if there
> are two.

A kitchen is a working room: a counter run along one or two walls, appliances, storage,
and a place to eat. Keep a clear floor strip to move through.

**Counter run** (L- or U-shaped against the walls):
- A continuous worktop of **slabs** (smooth_stone/quartz) or upside-down stairs at waist
  height, supported on a base of **barrels** (drawers) and the odd `cabinet` (a
  closed `barrel`/`chest`).
- A **cooktop / oven**: a **smoker** and/or **blast_furnace** set into the counter (lit),
  or a `campfire` under a hood of stairs as a stove.
- A **sink**: a `cauldron` with water set into the counter, a tap suggested by a `lever`
  or a `lightning_rod`/`end_rod` stub.
- A **prep surface**: a `crafting_table`, a `smithing_table`, or `fletching_table` reads as
  a butcher block.

**Storage:** overhead **shelves** (slabs on the wall, item frames showing food/tools),
wall-mounted barrels, a **pantry** corner of stacked barrels + composters (sacks of
produce), bottles via `glass_bottle` item frames.

**Dining nook:** a small **table** (fence post + pressure-plate/carpet top, or a slab
slab) with 1–2 **stair chairs**, a hanging `lantern` over it, a `potted` herb on the sill.
Light the work zone clearly (lanterns/glowstone behind slabs). Floors read well in
`*_planks` or a checker of two slab colours.
