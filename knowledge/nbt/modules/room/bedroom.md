# Room module — Bedroom

> Loads **only when the user assigns a Bedroom to a floor**. Guidance-only: build the
> furnishing yourself with ordinary ops inside the floor's interior (the "[Room plan]"
> lines give the floor's y range). Share the storey with the other assigned room if there
> are two.
>
> **Scale to the floor:** match the furniture to the room's size — the "[Room plan]" line
> names the space tier (snug / standard / grand) and a furnishing **preset** to base the
> layout on; build that preset in the house decoration's materials. Never leave a large
> room half-empty (see `14-furnishing-by-space.md`).

A bedroom is a single private room built around one made **bed**. Keep it calm and not
overstuffed — bed, bedside, storage, a window, light.

**Bed:**
- One `*_bed` against a wall (colour to suit the decoration). A bed is two cells — keep
  both clear and leave space to walk around at least one long side.
- A **headboard**: a row of upside-down stairs or a banner/painting on the wall above.

**Bedside & storage:**
- A **nightstand** each side (a `barrel`, a slab on a fence post, or a single block) with
  a `lantern`, `candle`, or a `flower_pot` on top.
- A **wardrobe / dresser** against another wall: stacked `barrel`s, a `chest`, framed
  doors (`*_door` pair or trapdoors) on a 2–3 wide cabinet.

**Comfort & dressing:**
- A **rug** beside the bed (small carpet rectangle).
- A **window** with "curtains" (banners or `*_wool`/trapdoor framing) — below grade use
  `iron_bars` instead of glass.
- Optional **desk** (slab top + supports) with a `lectern`/`candle`, or a small reading
  chair (stairs).

Light it softly — a lantern or two, or candles — enough to read by, not harsh.
