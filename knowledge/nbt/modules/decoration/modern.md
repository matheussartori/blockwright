# Decoration module — Modern

> Module guide for the **modern** decoration. It loads into the system prompt **only when
> the user picks the Modern decoration**. A decoration is the MATERIAL palette + mood; pair
> this with the **modern exterior form** (`form/modern.md`) for a quartz-and-glass villa.

## The palette

A clean, contemporary, NON-rustic material story — white, dark accent, warm wood, glass:

- **Primary mass:** `white_concrete` + `smooth_quartz` (walls, parapets, the bulk of every
  volume). Bright and matte. Quartz blocks/pillars for crisper feature walls.
- **Dark accents:** `polished_blackstone` / `deepslate` / `dark_oak_log` as vertical fins,
  columns, and trim that break up the white. Used sparingly but boldly.
- **Warm wood:** `stripped_dark_oak_log` / `spruce_planks` for decking, soffits, and accent
  cladding — the warmth against the white.
- **Glass:** lots of it — `glass` blocks and `glass_pane` in big uninterrupted curtain walls
  (see the form guide). Tinted/gray glass is fine for a darker look.
- **Flat roofs:** `smooth_quartz_slab` / `quartz_block` lids with a thin parapet — never a
  pitched roof material.
- **Light:** `sea_lantern` (recessed/flush, clean white light) and `end_rod` accents; hidden
  strip lighting under overhangs. Avoid the rustic hanging lantern as the main light.

## Mood

Crisp, minimal, bright, horizontal. **No decay, no moss, no cobblestone, no rough timber.**
Sharp edges and flat planes. Negative space (open terraces, double-height voids) is part of
the look. Greenery is manicured — `azalea`/leaf hedges, potted plants, a lawn — not wild.

## Avoid

- Cobblestone, mossy/cracked blocks, rough logs as the main wall — those read rustic, not modern.
- A warm honey-wood box (that's the `cozy` look) — modern is white + glass first.
- Cluttered many-colored palettes — hold to white + one dark accent + wood + glass.
