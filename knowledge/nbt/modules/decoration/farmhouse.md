# Decoration module — Farmhouse

> Module guide for the **farmhouse** decoration. It loads into the system prompt **only when
> the user picks the Farmhouse decoration**. A decoration is the MATERIAL palette + mood; it
> auto-pairs with the **Farmhouse structure type** (the cross-gabled L casco with the
> veranda), so this is the warm-oak + dark-roof half of a *casa de sítio*.

## The palette

A warm, rustic, country material story — honey oak, dark timber framing, stone, dark roof:

- **Walls / boarding:** `oak_planks` — honey-toned, the bulk of the facade.
- **Timber framing:** `stripped_dark_oak_log` — vertical corner posts, a mid-wall belt
  course, and battens crossing the oak boards (the Tudor-style dark frame is the signature).
  `stripped_oak_log` as a lighter secondary accent.
- **Base / plinth:** `cobblestone` (with `mossy_cobblestone` / `cobblestone_slab` for the
  water table) — the house sits on stone, never bare timber on soil.
- **Roof:** **dark and tiled** — `deepslate_tile_stairs` + `deepslate_tile_slab` ridge.
  Steep, with an overhanging eave + fascia. Never a light or thatch roof.
- **Windows:** `glass_pane` in wide bands, slab sills, `spruce_trapdoor` / `dark_oak`
  shutters framing them.
- **Doors / rails:** `oak_door`; `oak_fence` for the porch balustrade + upper gallery rail.
- **Light:** warm hanging `lantern` along the porch, gallery and eaves.
- **Greenery:** ivy (`vine`) on corners, flower boxes under windows, potted plants + hay on
  the porch — the lived-in, worked-land feel.

## Mood

Warm, lived-in, grounded, a little weathered. Honey wood against dark frame against grey
stone, under a dark roof. Cosy and worked — a home on a farm, not a showpiece.

## Avoid

- A flush single-material wall — the dark framing + stone plinth against the oak is the point.
- A light/orange roof — keep it **dark** (deepslate tile).
- Quartz/white/glass-heavy surfaces (that's the `modern` look), or a ruined/abandoned feel.
