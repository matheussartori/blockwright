# Decoration module — Gothic

> Module guide for the **gothic** decoration. It loads into the system prompt **only when
> the user picks the Gothic decoration**. A decoration is the MATERIAL palette + mood; it
> auto-pairs with the **Gothic structure type** (the towered, portico'd manor), so this is
> the black-with-white-detailing half of a gothic manor.

## The palette

A brooding, vertical material story — black timber + blackstone picked out with pale stone:

- **Walls:** `dark_oak_planks` framed by `dark_oak_log` quoins — **predominantly black**,
  the bulk of the facade.
- **Detailing (the white):** `polished_diorite` (or `calcite`/`diorite`) belt courses at the
  floor lines, a cornice, window **sills + jambs**, and the entry surround — the pale picking
  out against the black is the signature contrast.
- **Base / pillars:** `polished_blackstone` + `polished_blackstone_bricks` footings and the
  portico columns.
- **Roof:** **near-black slate** — `deepslate_tile_stairs` + `deepslate_tile_slab` ridge.
  **Steep** and sharp, with an overhang. Never light or shallow.
- **Chapel glass:** `gray_stained_glass` for the side wall of tall windows; `glass_pane`
  lancets elsewhere, with dark mullions.
- **Doors / rails:** `dark_oak_door`; `dark_oak_fence` / `iron_bars` for balustrades.
- **Light:** `soul_lantern` (cold blue) — the eerie, manorly cast.

## Mood

Brooding, vertical, asymmetric, cold. Black against a few sharp white lines, under a steep
slate roof, lit blue. A haunted-looking manor — tall and severe, never warm or cozy.

## Avoid

- Warm/light materials or a shallow roof — keep everything **black** with only **white**
  accents, the pitch **steep**.
- Flooding it with white — black must dominate; the pale stone is a DETAIL, not the wall.
- Pink/cherry (that's `sakura`) or quartz/white-glass (that's `modern`).
