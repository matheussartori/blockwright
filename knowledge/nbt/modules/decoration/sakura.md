# Decoration module — Sakura

> Module guide for the **sakura** decoration. It loads into the system prompt **only when
> the user picks the Sakura decoration**. A decoration is the MATERIAL palette + mood; it
> auto-pairs with the **Sakura structure type** (the raised blossom cottage), so this is the
> pink-cherry half of a cherry-blossom home.

## The palette

A soft, romantic, springtime material story — pink cherry over pale stone, blossoms:

- **Walls / cladding:** `cherry_planks` — soft pink, the bulk of the facade.
- **Posts / framing:** `cherry_log` corner posts + `stripped_cherry_log` beams.
- **Base / plinth:** `stone_bricks` (with `stone_brick_slab`/`stairs` for the water table) —
  the cottage sits raised on a **visible** pale-stone basement, never timber on soil.
- **Roof:** **pink** — `cherry_stairs` + `cherry_slab` ridge. A steep gable with a 1-block
  overhang. Keep it pink (NOT dark slate, NOT red) so the blossom reads against it.
- **Windows:** `glass_pane` in bands, `cherry_slab` sills, with **`cherry_leaves` window
  boxes** under them.
- **Doors / rails:** `cherry_door`; `cherry_fence` for the balcony + entry-stair rails.
- **Blossom:** `cherry_leaves` (foliage) draped over eaves + cascading down corners +
  `pink_petals`/flower pots — the signature material.
- **Light:** warm hanging `lantern` from the eaves and balcony.

## Mood

Soft, romantic, springtime, gentle. Pink wood against pale stone under a pink roof, framed
by blossom boughs. A storybook cottage in a cherry grove — bright and tender, never gloomy.

## Avoid

- A dark or red roof — keep it **pink** (cherry).
- Burying the stone base — it should READ as a raised, visible lower storey.
- Blackstone/dark-oak gloom (that's `gothic`) or quartz/white/glass (that's `modern`).
