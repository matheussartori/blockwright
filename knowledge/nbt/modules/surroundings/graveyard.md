# Surroundings module — Gothic graveyard

> Module guide for the **graveyard** surroundings ring (gothic manor only). It loads into
> the system prompt **only when the user picks the Graveyard surroundings**. The ring is
> CODE-BUILT and LOCKED (part of the seeded shell): the whole cemetery already exists when
> you start — your job is to ENRICH it with atmospheric detail, never to rebuild, re-wall
> or cover it.

## What is already there (do not remove or cover)

The build box is MUCH larger than the manor: the grounds are deliberately grand (around
four times a normal yard) and FRONT-heavy — the long approach in front of the entrance is
the showpiece. The lawn is clipped to a chamfered, irregular outline, so the plot is never
a plain rectangle. Everything is laid from the ground level (y=0 of the box):

- A **crumbling perimeter wall** following a varied (chamfered-corner) outline: a mossy
  stone-brick course capped with cobblestone-wall battlements, **broken open in places**
  (gaps and uneven height) and punctuated by **stone piers carrying soul lanterns** — the
  eerie churchyard light.
- An **arched stone gate** in the front wall, aligned with the manor entrance: tall piers,
  a stair arch + keystone, a cobblestone threshold and iron-bar leaves standing aside.
- A wide **gravel approach** from the gate to the door, a **cross path** (transept) across
  the front grounds, and a **path looping the manor**.
- **Rows of headstones** flanking the approach — cobblestone-wall markers, tall mossy
  monuments and leaning broken stones over disturbed-earth (podzol) mounds with slab
  ledgers.
- A **ruined colonnade** of toppling stone-brick pillars (varied height, some collapsed to
  rubble, broken capitals, ivy creeping over them).
- A great **weeping tree** (oak trunk + broad canopy + trailing leaf strands) as the
  focal point.
- A small stone **mausoleum/crypt** with a slab roof and an iron-grate door, plus seeded
  **rubble piles**, and overgrowth (**ferns, poppies, leaf clumps**) reclaiming the lawn.

## What to add (atmospheric detail only)

- **More graves + memorials**: extra headstones (walls/stairs/signs as inscriptions),
  flower pots and wreaths on the mounds, candles (the odd lit campfire or candle), a
  cracked sarcophagus (double slab over a closed lid).
- **Ruins + age**: more mossy/cracked stone rubble, a fallen pillar lying flat, vines or
  persistent leaves trailing down walls and the colonnade, cobwebs in the crypt.
- **Planting + atmosphere**: more ferns/tall grass and poppies on open grass, a second
  small tree, lily pads or a murky pond in a low corner, a low iron fence around a family
  plot.
- **Lighting**: a few extra soul lanterns on wall piers, lamp posts (cobblestone-wall +
  lantern) or beside the crypt if a corner reads dark — match the existing posts, never
  floating light sources.

## Rules

- **Never remove or alter** the perimeter wall, the gate, the paths, the headstone rows,
  the colonnade, the weeping tree or the crypt — only add around and between them.
- **Keep the gate→door approach clear** end to end — nothing placed on the path cells.
- Verticality is allowed here (ruined columns, the tree, the crypt, the gate arch), but
  keep every new feature **inside the build box's height** and OPEN-AIR — never roof or
  floor over the cemetery grounds.
- **Leaves and plants must be `persistent: true`** (placed foliage decays in-game
  otherwise).
- Keep the palette **gothic and weathered** — mossy/cracked stone, cobblestone, iron,
  soul lanterns, dead/oak foliage — never bright or modern materials.
- The manor's interior/facade work follows the structure's own guide — this guide only
  governs the cemetery ring OUTSIDE the walls.
